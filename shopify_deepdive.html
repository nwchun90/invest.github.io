business model — two revenue engines
Subscription Solutions (~27% of revenue)
Monthly recurring fees: Basic ($29), Shopify ($79), Advanced ($299), Plus (~$2K+/mo). ~5.6M merchants. MRR: $178M in Q4 2025, growing ~17–20% YoY. Gross margin ~80% — the high-quality anchor.
Extremely sticky — migrating between platforms disrupts the entire merchant operation (domain, payments, apps, analytics). Platform switching costs are among the highest in SaaS.
Merchant Solutions (~73% of revenue)
Shopify Payments, Shop Pay, Shopify Capital (lending), Shipping, Fulfillment. Revenue scales directly with GMV — the bigger merchants grow, the more Shopify earns. +35% YoY. Gross margin ~38%.
The infrastructure play: as GMV scales, FCF compounds faster than revenue due to operating leverage. Shop Pay 200M+ users, 50% higher conversion than guest checkout. Now processing transactions off-platform internationally.
The AI Commerce Layer — Emerging Third Engine
Catalog API + MCP: Enables third-party AI agents (OpenAI, Alphabet, etc.) to interact directly with Shopify stores — processing full purchase journeys. Orders from AI searches grew 15× in 12 months to January 2026. Shopify becomes the checkout layer for AI-native commerce.
B2B hypergrowth: B2B GMV +96% in 2025. Winter '26 Edition shipped 150+ AI/B2B updates including ERP integrations (NetSuite, SAP, Oracle), EDI purchase order sync, and partial fulfillment payments — targeting the wholesale and enterprise segment.
Commerce Components: Large enterprises (Nike, Mattel) embed individual Shopify modules into their own systems via API — a Stripe-like B2B model that expands TAM beyond the 5.6M traditional merchants to Fortune 500 companies.
competitor quantitative scorecard

How to read the scorecard

EBITDA & margin: Adjusted/non-GAAP EBITDA used for all three companies to strip out stock-based compensation and restructuring charges, enabling a like-for-like operating comparison. Higher margin = more efficient conversion of revenue to earnings before capex.

Altman Z-Score: Bankruptcy prediction model. Score >2.99 = safe zone. For high-growth tech companies the score is inflated by high market cap vs. liabilities — treat as a relative, not absolute, measure. All three companies are solvent; the spread reflects financial strength.

Piotroski F-Score: 9-point binary checklist covering profitability (4 signals), leverage/liquidity (3 signals), and operational efficiency (2 signals). Score 8–9 = strong; 5–7 = moderate; 0–4 = weak. Each point is either 0 or 1.

CCC & EVA: Cash Conversion Cycle measures how fast the business converts operations into cash (negative = collects before paying, excellent for SaaS). Economic Value Added = NOPAT minus the cost of capital deployed — positive EVA means the business creates wealth above its cost of capital.

investment case — bull vs. bear
Bull case
Compounding FCF: $2.01B in 2025, guided higher. At $127, FCF yield ~1.3% is modest but grows rapidly — TIKR models $2.5B by 2026E. The buyback reduces share count, compounding per-share growth.
AI tailwind: Orders from AI searches 15× in 12 months. OpenAI and Alphabet integrations via Catalog API and MCP position SHOP as the checkout infrastructure for AI-native commerce — an entirely new revenue category.
B2B runway: +96% GMV in 2025, from a small base. The wholesale market is estimated at $20T+ globally vs. ~$6T B2C — Shopify has barely scratched this surface.
Consensus: 38 Buy, 8 Hold, 1 Sell. Avg target $160.15 implies +26% from today. Piotroski 8/9, Z-Score 8.2, EVA positive $389M — all superior to peers.
Bear case
Valuation premium: Trailing P/E 119×, PEG 9.56×. Even with 30% growth, any macro shock that slows GMV will compress the multiple sharply. The stock needs flawless execution.
Q1 FCF guided lower: Q1 2026 FCF margin guided "low-to-mid teens" vs. 19% in Q4 2025 — signalling increased AI capex. Margins may dip before recovering.
Death cross + negative volume balance: Institutional sellers still more active than buyers. Today's rally needs follow-through confirmation above $129.31.
Tariff exposure: Many Shopify merchants source from China/SE Asia. 2026 tariff escalation may dampen GMV growth in affected categories — a risk not fully priced in.
verdict summary
Shopify is the highest-quality commerce infrastructure business in the public markets — a Piotroski 8/9, positive EVA, 30%-growth compounder with $2B annual FCF and a near-monopoly position among high-GMV merchants. Today's +8.31% rally is a meaningful technical development: price is now testing the critical $129.31 resistance for the first time in weeks, supported by above-average volume and a MACD buy crossover. The macro catalyst (Iran peace-talk signals, NASDAQ rally) is real but temporary — what matters most is whether Shopify's May 2026 earnings confirm Q1 revenue in the "low-30s%" range as guided.

For a long-term portfolio (3–5 years), the fundamental case is clear: 30% revenue growth at $11.56B scale is exceptionally rare, and the AI commerce tailwind (15× AI-search order growth) is just beginning to be monetised. The competitor scorecard shows Shopify decisively outperforming Wix and BigCommerce across every quantitative dimension.

Current positioning: Today's session at $127.41 is more actionable than any point in the past 3 months. A sustained close above $129.31 on continued volume this week would confirm a technical trend reversal. For a patient long-term investor, accumulating in the $120–$128 range before May earnings represents a favourable risk/reward setup.
