the three strategies — rules and rationale
MODEL 1 · DCA
Dollar Cost Averaging
The most widely practised strategy. You invest a fixed dollar amount every month, unconditionally. Over time, you naturally buy more units at low prices and fewer at high prices — without ever having to think about it.
Start: C$1,000
Every month: + C$100 (fixed)
Dividends: reinvested
5-yr cash total: C$7,000
10-yr cash total: C$13,000
Best for: investors who want a set-and-forget plan with a predictable, budget-friendly monthly commitment.
MODEL 2 · VA
Value Averaging
A disciplined contrarian approach. Each month, top up the portfolio to meet a rising value target. Bull markets require no new money; bear markets trigger heavier buying. The result is systematically higher capital efficiency than DCA.
Start: C$1,000
Month 1 target: C$1,200
Month 2 target: C$1,400 (+ C$200 each month)
Invest: only enough to reach the target
Dividends: reinvested
Best for: disciplined investors who can monitor monthly and tolerate variable cash outlays. Achieves the highest return per dollar invested.
MODEL 3 · GOLDEN PULSE
Golden Pulse Strategy
A price-reactive strategy with no fixed monthly commitment. Check the price against your average cost each month. Large dips trigger aggressive buying. Significant breakouts prompt a modest top-up. Sideways markets are held through silently.
Start: C$1,000
If price < avg cost: buy C$1,000 (dip buyer)
If price = avg to +50%: no action (hold)
If price ≥ avg cost × 1.5: buy C$100 (momentum)
Dividends: reinvested
Best for: investors who closely follow the market and keep a capital reserve ready. Builds the largest absolute portfolio — but demands patience and flexible liquidity.

four acts — the ZGD story across ten years
ACT 01 · APR 2016 – DEC 2018
Bear market — the opportunity window
ZGD fell from C$26 to C$10.80 — a 58% collapse driven by rising US rates and declining gold miner profitability. DCA and VA kept buying through the bottom. Golden Pulse fired its C$1,000 dip-buyer mode repeatedly. All three strategies were building at cheap prices that defined their long-term returns.
ACT 02 · JAN 2019 – DEC 2020
Recovery and Covid — volatility rewarded
ZGD surged from C$14 to C$55 (292%). The March 2020 Covid crash briefly took it back to C$17 before an explosive recovery. This was the first period where portfolios built during the 2017-2018 bear market began to shine. Dividends resumed in December 2020 — the DRIP compounding cycle begins.
ACT 03 · JAN 2021 – DEC 2023
Consolidation — patience tested
ZGD oscillated between C$22 and C$46, frustrating investors after the 2020 surge. Dividends accelerated dramatically (+96% in 2022, +7.7% in 2023) as gold miners posted record profits. VA contributed minimally in strong months; Golden Pulse held patiently in sideways periods, accumulating dividend-reinvested units.
ACT 04 · JAN 2024 – APR 2026
Breakout — ten years of patience rewarded
ZGD launched from C$38 to C$337 (787%) in just 27 months. The US-Iran war in February 2026 drove gold to US$5,602 ATH. Every unit accumulated during the 2016-2023 building phase — cheap, dividend-reinvested, patiently held — was now worth multiples of its cost. This is why systematic investing works.
how to read the % return chart

Why percentage return — not absolute value — is the right comparison

The fairness problem: If Golden Pulse invests C$18,000 and DCA invests C$13,000, a higher ending value for Pulse tells you almost nothing. Of course more money in produces more money out. The only honest comparison is return per dollar invested.

What the chart shows: Each line represents (portfolio value ÷ total cash invested − 1) × 100. If DCA shows +1,200%, you turned every C$1 you put in into C$13. If VA shows +2,023%, you turned every C$1 into C$21.23 — a dramatically more efficient outcome despite similar ending values.

The VA paradox: VA often shows higher % return precisely because it invests less in bull markets. When ZGD doubled in a month, VA put in nothing — but still captured the gain on existing units. DCA kept deploying at expensive prices, diluting its own return rate.

When Pulse leads on %: Golden Pulse's % return spikes after large dip-buying episodes. Buying C$1,000 near the bottom (when the average cost is low) and then watching the market recover creates outsized return ratios. Its % return temporarily lags DCA during the buying phase, then surges during recoveries.

key considerations before you start
ZGD's 0.60% MER is the all-in cost — no trading commissions in a TFSA or RRSP, no currency conversion, and no withholding tax on dividends from a Canadian-domiciled fund. The total expense is 60 cents per C$100 invested per year.
Value Averaging requires cash reserves — in a severe bear market, VA may demand large contributions to meet its target. Investors should maintain a dedicated account balance (minimum 3–6 months of VA target increments, or C$600–C$1,200 in this model) to fund unexpected large buys.
Golden Pulse fires most aggressively in bear markets — investors need genuine liquidity ready to deploy C$1,000 during a drawdown. This is precisely when confidence is lowest. The strategy only works if you can follow the rules without emotion.
ZGD experienced a 60% maximum drawdown in 2015 — all three strategies would have seen severe unrealised losses at that point. DRIP and continued systematic investing are what turn that drawdown into the foundation of the subsequent +1,200% gain.
Gold miners are highly cyclical — ZGD has no place as the only holding in a portfolio. These simulations assume it is one component of a diversified strategy that also includes Canadian equity (XIU), bonds (ZAG), and cash. The systematic approach reduces timing risk but does not eliminate sector concentration risk.
verdict
All three strategies beat a simple buy-and-hold approach — the systematic discipline of regular investment eliminates the temptation to time the market and ensures participation in every recovery. Value Averaging wins decisively on capital efficiency: it deploys roughly the same total as DCA over ten years while generating a far superior return on invested capital. Dollar Cost Averaging is the right choice for the vast majority of investors — it is simple, requires no monitoring, and turned C$13,000 into C$169,000 over a decade. Golden Pulse is the contrarian's engine: it builds the largest absolute portfolio for those with the discipline and liquidity to follow through during deep drawdowns.

Implementation note: For a Canadian investor, execute all three inside a TFSA — growth and withdrawals are fully tax-free. Use the DRIP feature if your broker supports it on ZGD, or reinvest the December distribution manually in January at the prevailing price.
