what happened — the collapse narrative
GDDY peaked at $193.55 in early 2025 riding AI/Airo optimism. On Feb 25, 2026, Q4 earnings beat EPS and revenue estimates — but 2026 revenue guidance of $5.20–5.28B missed consensus by ~$10M (tiny in dollar terms, huge in signal terms). The stock fell -15.6% in a single day — the largest S&P 500 decline that session — as a wave of analyst target cuts followed. The market re-rated GDDY from a high-growth AI story to a mature, slower-growth cash flow business. It hasn't recovered.

Domain moat — durable but eroding
Near-monopoly at 82–89M domains, 3× Namecheap. Domain→site→payments funnel unmatched at scale with 20.4M customers.
Lost 1.25M .com domains YoY while Namecheap gained 1.86M. Renewal price gap ($22 vs $18.48) widening.

AI / Airo platform — promise vs. delivery
25 AI agents live on Airo.ai. Multi-model stack (OpenAI, Claude, Llama via AWS Bedrock). ANS integrated with Salesforce MuleSoft Agent Fabric.
Net new customers essentially flat (+4K in Q4 despite Airo). 6% revenue guidance shows AI not yet bending the growth curve.
Independent reviews rank GoDaddy's AI output quality below Wix ADI and 10Web. RBC applies "AI-discounted multiple."
supply chain & infrastructure dependencies
Cloud/AI compute: Heavy AWS Bedrock usage for AI workloads. As Airo agents scale from 25 toward 100+, inference spend on OpenAI/Anthropic APIs is a rising, undisclosed cost line.
ICANN/registry chain: GoDaddy holds ICANN registrar accreditation and operates its own GoDaddy Registry — giving upstream control of TLD supply and pricing that smaller players lack.
Payments stack: GoDaddy Payments competes with Stripe/Square. Settlement infrastructure relies on Visa/Mastercard card networks — subject to interchange rule changes.
Leverage risk: Aggressive buybacks ($3.2B+) have increased net debt while growth slows. Interest expense is a rising cost against a decelerating top line.

can the $193 high ever be reclaimed?
Bull case — yes, it is possible
FCF machine: $1.61B today → $1.80B guided 2026 → models show $2.57B by 2030. At $80, the FCF yield is ~15% — extraordinarily cheap for a near-monopoly registrar.
ARPU flywheel: High-value customers (>$500/yr) grew 11% YoY. If Airo monetisation inflects and ARPU reaches $350+, revenue re-accelerates without needing new customers.
ANS platform layer: The Salesforce MuleSoft integration extends GoDaddy into enterprise AI agent identity — a new platform layer no current competitor owns at SMB scale.
Margin compounding + buybacks: 1,000bps EBITDA expansion over 5 years. Share count declining — EPS compounds faster than revenue.
Bear case — the $193 high may be structurally gone
Customer shrinkage: GoDaddy's $193 price required a growth narrative. Customer count declining (-0.4% YoY) removes the forward multiple expansion that drove the ATH.
AI as disruptor: AI builders commoditise GoDaddy's most profitable upsells. Cloudflare, ChatGPT plugins, and no-code tools undercut from below.
6% ceiling: Returning to $193 at current multiples would require ~$7–8B revenue by 2030 — implying 10%+ CAGR from today's 6% guided growth. Achievable only with a major AI monetisation inflection.
Leverage trap: $3.2B+ in buybacks increased net debt as growth slows. Compresses multiples further and limits strategic flexibility for M&A.

verdict summary
GoDaddy today is a value stock masquerading as a fallen growth stock. The business is fundamentally healthy — 32% EBITDA margins, $1.6B FCF, a near-monopoly registrar position, and strong ARPU growth. But the market has re-rated it as a mature, 6%-growth business, stripping away the AI premium that drove the $193 ATH.

The ATH is not permanently lost — but recovering it requires Airo to demonstrably accelerate revenue growth (not just margins) within 2–3 quarters. April 30 earnings is the first real test. If A&C bookings growth closes the gap with A&C revenue growth, and net customer adds return positive, the narrative can shift. If not, GDDY risks becoming a value trap: cheap on FCF, structurally ex-growth.

Current positioning: The stock at ~$80, 15% FCF yield, 12.87× P/E, and 68.8% below Simply Wall St fair value is pricing in the worst case. For a long-term investor, this is a high-quality business at an unusual discount — but it requires patience and conviction that AI can be a tailwind, not just a headwind.
