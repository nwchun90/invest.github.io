How to read this chart

Why start at zero? Gold costs $4,720 per ounce while an AU share costs $90. If we plotted the actual prices on one chart, gold would tower over AU and you'd see nothing useful. Starting both at 0% on the same date lets us compare how much each moved, not what they cost.

Why does AU move so much more? AngloGold mines gold at roughly $1,450 per ounce and sells it at the market price. When gold rises by $100, their profit per ounce rises by the full $100 — that's enormous leverage. A 1% move in gold can mean 3–5% in AU's share price.

What happened in March 2026? The US-Iran war began February 28. Paradoxically, rising oil prices (Iran blocked the Strait of Hormuz) raised inflation fears, which made investors worry the US Federal Reserve would keep rates high — bad for gold. AU fell even harder due to its leverage, dropping 38% from peak while gold fell 17%.

What does the gap now mean? Gold has recovered more than AU relative to their war-era lows. That gap represents potential upside for AU if gold stays elevated — and with a new 4.9 million ounce gold reserve announced in Nevada (low cost at $954/oz to mine), AU has company-specific catalysts too.

ACT 01 · APR–OCT 2025
The great rerating — AU wakes up
Gold climbed steadily from $3,100 to $4,400, rising 42%. But AU exploded from $32 to $110 — a 244% gain. The market was finally pricing in that AU's earnings had tripled on the same production volume, thanks to margin leverage. Central banks globally bought 863 tonnes of gold in 2025 — historically elevated demand.
ACT 02 · NOV 2025–FEB 2026
Gold hits $5,600 — AU peaks at $129
Gold reached an all-time high of $5,602 on January 28, 2026, driven by tariff uncertainty, Middle East tensions, and record ETF inflows. AU matched it, reaching its own all-time high of $129.14 on March 2. Gold was up 80% from our April 2025 base; AU was up 303%. The leverage had fully expressed itself.
ACT 03 · MAR 2026
War, correction, and the CEO shock
The US-Iran war began February 28. Rising oil prices from the Hormuz blockade triggered inflation fears and a gold selloff. AU fell harder: CEO departure rumours on March 19 added a company-specific blow. AU bottomed at $80 — down 38% from peak. Gold only fell 17% from its high. The leverage worked against AU here.
ACT 04 · TODAY, APR 1 2026
Recovery underway — gap remains
Gold has bounced back to $4,720 (+52% from 1yr ago). AU has recovered to $90 (+181% from 1yr ago). But AU is still 30% below its March 2 peak while gold is only 16% below its January 28 peak. This gap suggests AU still has room to catch up — especially with a newly announced Nevada gold mine and Q1 earnings due May 8.
