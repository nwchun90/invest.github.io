How to Read Wealth Creation

The Profit Mirage: Standard Net Income or EBITDA can be positive while EVA is negative. If a company doesn't earn more than its WACC, it is effectively a "zombie" destroying shareholder value over time.

Speed as a Moat: A shrinking Cash Conversion Cycle is often a better signal of dominance than revenue growth. It indicates the business can self-fund its own expansion without dilution or debt.

Safety Convergence: The ideal long-term compounder shows a rising F-Score (improving quality) while maintaining a Z-Score above 3.0 (financial integrity).

Wealth Creation Signal: The ultimate goal is identifying positive EVA growth. This represents the "surplus value" that drives market-beating returns over full economic cycles.
